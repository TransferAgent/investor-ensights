### Why Zero-Knowledge Proofs Matter and Why NOIR is the Game-Changer for Tableicity
[San Francisco, CA] — In the high-stakes world of equity management and cap table security, privacy isn’t just a feature—it’s a non-negotiable. Enter Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKPs), a cryptographic breakthrough that allows companies to prove critical facts about their data without ever revealing the data itself. At Tableicity, we’re taking this revolutionary technology to the next level with NOIR, our internal Zero-Knowledge Evidence Engine, designed to redefine trust, compliance, and control for founders, investors, and regulators alike. Here’s why ZKPs matter and why NOIR is the cornerstone of our mission to disrupt legacy platforms like Carta and Cake Equity.

#### 1. The Privacy Crisis in Equity Management
Traditional cap table platforms operate on a “public-by-default” model, where sensitive ownership data—names, share counts, and financial stakes—is stored in centralized databases accessible to platform admins, employees, or even regulators with the right login. This exposure is a ticking time bomb for stealth startups, high-net-worth investors, and pre-IPO companies who cannot afford their portfolios to be scraped by competitors or exposed in data breaches. Carta’s 2024 secondaries controversy, as reported by TechCrunch, is a stark reminder that even the optics of privileged access can shatter market trust.

Zero-Knowledge Proofs solve this crisis at its core. Unlike conventional encryption (like SHA-256, which creates a verifiable “fingerprint” of data), ZKPs allow a company to prove a statement—such as “This investor owns at least 10% of the company”—without disclosing the raw numbers or identities behind it. This is the “Privacy Shield” that Tableicity is weaponizing through NOIR, ensuring that sensitive equity data remains invisible even to us as the platform provider.

#### 2. Why ZKPs Are a Strategic Imperative
For startups and high-growth companies navigating fundraising, audits, and regulatory scrutiny, ZKPs offer transformative benefits that directly address pain points in today’s equity management landscape:
- **Selective Disclosure**: Founders can prove to potential lead investors that their cap table is clean or that no single entity owns more than 20%, all without revealing the identities or stakes of other shareholders. This builds trust without sacrificing privacy.
- **Trustless Audits**: Auditors can verify that the sum of all shares equals 100% or that transactions comply with company bylaws without ever accessing personal or financial details. This minimizes exposure during due diligence.
- **Regulatory Compliance**: ZKPs enable companies to demonstrate to regulators that KYC (Know Your Customer) checks have been performed on all investors without sharing private ID documents, aligning with stringent SEC and FINRA requirements.
- **On-Chain Privacy**: For companies exploring blockchain integrations, ZKPs (like zk-SNARKs) can hide transaction amounts and participant identities while still allowing networks to confirm the validity of every transfer.

These capabilities aren’t just technical niceties—they’re a competitive moat. In a market where global deal activity has dropped 19% YoY, founders need every edge to signal professionalism and security to wary investors. ZKPs, powered by NOIR, position Tableicity as the only cap table platform that offers true “Mathematical Certainty” without compromise.

#### 3. NOIR: Tableicity’s Zero-Knowledge Evidence Engine
NOIR isn’t just a tool; it’s the beating heart of Tableicity’s privacy-first mission. Built as an internal proving module with a clean abstraction boundary inside our application stack, NOIR leverages open-source NoirJS technology to deliver browser-based, client-side privacy preservation. Here’s how it works and why it’s a game-changer:
- **The “Invisible” Process**: When a founder enters sensitive data—such as an investor’s name or share count—into Tableicity, NOIR operates locally on their device. It transforms this data into a cryptographic commitment, generating a ZK-proof that verifies specific facts (e.g., “This transaction is valid”) without ever uploading readable data to our servers in Germany or AWS in the US.
- **The Output**: What reaches Tableicity’s cloud isn’t a cap table—it’s a vault of mathematical proofs. These proofs can be verified by auditors, investors, or regulators through a simple click, confirming the truth of a statement without exposing the underlying equity state.
- **The Money Maker**: We don’t monetize data storage; we monetize trust. Every time an external party needs to confirm a proof—whether it’s an auditor validating compliance or an investor checking ownership—Tableicity charges for the verification event. We’re selling certainty, not access.

NOIR’s initial circuits are laser-focused on immediate commercial value, aligning with our transfer-agent and compliance narrative:
- **Proof of Ownership Threshold**: Proves an investor owns at least X shares or Y% on a fully diluted basis without revealing the rest of the cap table.
- **Proof of Dilution/Change**: Confirms a financing round or issuance shifts a holder’s stake from one threshold to another without disclosing unrelated positions.
- **Proof of Transfer Validity/Compliance**: Validates that a transfer adheres to company rules, authorized share limits, and ledger consistency, all while keeping data private.
