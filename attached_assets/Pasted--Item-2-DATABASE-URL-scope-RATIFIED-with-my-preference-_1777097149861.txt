All three reasons are technically correct. None of them address my actual concern.

My concern is not blast radius. My concern is observability hygiene during a green-field POC. Specifically:

Why I prefer Replit dev URL for Gate 1:

Audit log pollution. Every Gate 1 dry-run produces real newsroom_agent_runs rows, real newsroom_pipeline_jobs rows, and real newsroom_review_queue rows. Even with the dryRun=true filter chip, that's noise in prod's audit history. When Gate 4 lands the first real Worcester article, I want a clean audit signal — not "scroll past 50 fixture jobs to find the real one."

Replit dev URL exists for exactly this purpose. The shell is a Next.js app on Replit; dev/preview URLs are zero-cost to spin up and tear down. Refusing to use them for plumbing verification is not using the platform's affordances.

"Gate 0 closed against prod" is a non-sequitur. Gate 0 closed the contract. The contract is environment-agnostic. The same v1.1 endpoints exist on dev and prod. Where we exercise the contract during Gate 1 is an operational choice, not a contractual one.

The App Builder's argument cuts the other way at Gate 4. If dryRun=true is sufficient safety to run Gate 1 against prod, then why does Gate 4 need its own gate? Because at Gate 4 we transition from "fixture data in prod" to "real article in prod" and the audit trail must be clean enough to read. Polluting it with 2 days of Gate 1 dry-runs makes Gate 4 review harder.

HOWEVER — I am open to being wrong here. Two scenarios where the App Builder is right and I am wrong:

Scenario A: There is no working Replit dev URL for the shell. Spinning one up costs the App Builder more than 1 hour. In that case, prod with dryRun=true is the correct pragmatic call.
Scenario B: The App Builder has already implemented a dry-run-row sweep mechanism (cron job, admin button, anything that purges dryRun=true rows from newsroom_agent_runs and newsroom_review_queue after Gate 1 closes). In that case, prod is fine because the audit pollution is recoverable.

Architect needs one of three answers: (1) dev URL is here, use it; (2) dev URL not feasible, sweep mechanism in place, use prod; (3) neither — Architect rules prod with a logged commitment that App Builder builds the sweep before Gate 4 opens."
